About

Introduction

I am a robotics engineer and researcher with a background spanning embedded systems, computer vision, autonomous navigation, and machine learning. My work sits at the boundary between engineered systems and biological intelligence, building robots that see, hear, and adapt, while drawing inspiration from how living systems process information. My research interests lean toward the unconventional: event-based vision and neuromorphic sensing, lifetime adaptation in autonomous systems, uninformed reactive navigation, bioacoustics and non-human communication, and what machine learning can reveal about collective biological behaviour.

MSc Thesis · arXiv 2026

BeeVE, Unsupervised Honeybee Acoustic State Discovery

VQ-VAE applied to PaSST embeddings of honeybee recordings. A discrete acoustic codebook separates queen-right from queen-less colonies without labels and identifies potential hidden acoustic sub-states.
